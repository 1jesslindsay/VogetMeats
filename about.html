About Us

Our Story

The Voget Meats legacy was born from the hardships of late 19th century Germany, and a strong desire for freedom of entrepreneurship. Born in 1888, Garfield Voget immigrated to the US in the early 20th century and eventually settled in Hubbard, Oregon, circa 1918. Originally operating a co-op creamery at Voget's modern site, Garfield eventually bought out co-op members and slowly expanded his business model to include lard rendering, and carcass processing.

Responding to a rapid increase in the demand for custom processing and smoking, particularly in pork, Garfield founded Voget Meats in 1940 to serve the local agricultural community. Armed with old-world recipes and traditions, Garfield again expanded his businesses services to include retail products and increased smoking capacity in 1952.

Since that time, Voget Meats has undergone several expansions, including the building of the modern retail store in 1971. Today, Voget Meats remains locally owned and operated, and proudly celebrates Garfield's legacy with quality old-fashioned smoked meats, including award winning hams, bacon, sausage, and much more. We additionally specialize in retail fresh meats, custom wild-game processing, and are renowned for our holiday hams and smoked turkeys.
